Question answering systems built on large language models (LLMs) inherit a snapshot of the world. As facts evolve, answers that were once correct degrade, and naively dated responses can harm user trust. Prior work on temporal QA and time-aware modeling has flagged this gap: datasets for time-sensitive questions \citep{chen2021datasetansweringtimesensitivequestions}, temporal knowledge bases for LMs \citep{10.1162/tacl_a_00459}, real-time QA benchmarks \citep{kasai2024realtimeqawhatsanswer}, search-augmented refreshing of LMs \citep{vu-etal-2024-freshllms}, and self-updating present-anchored QA suites \citep{meem-etal-2024-pat} all underline that factuality tests often ignore how quickly answers drift. We refer to this requirement as the recency demand of a question.

We revisit recency-aware evaluation with a focus on how temporal dynamics differ across questions. Some answers change slowly and predictably (stationary behavior), while others depend on volatile or event-driven contexts (non-stationary behavior). Questions may hinge on one event or weave together multiple events whose timing or causal order matters. Capturing these distinctions is essential both for dataset design and for choosing the right retrieval or updating strategy at inference time.

This paper introduces a new recency-focused dataset and generation pipeline with four key improvements over existing recency QA resources: (1) a richer taxonomy that combines stationarity with single- vs. multi-event dependencies, adding causal and purely temporal multi-event variants; (2) a prompt-driven pipeline that produces and labels 600 questions, each with recency classes, stationarity flags, and multi-event markers; (3) perturbation-based augmentation that creates harder, minimally edited temporal variants to stress LLM robustness; and (4) an external + human validation pass (Gemini API and manual review) that filters malformed items and fixes labels. Together, these components yield a larger, more diverse, and more challenging testbed for studying when answers change, not just what the answers are. Relative to prior time-sensitive datasets \citep{chen2021datasetansweringtimesensitivequestions}, temporal KB-aware modeling \citep{10.1162/tacl_a_00459}, real-time QA \citep{kasai2024realtimeqawhatsanswer}, search-augmented refreshing \citep{vu-etal-2024-freshllms}, and self-updating present-anchored QA \citep{meem-etal-2024-pat}, we contribute explicit recency labels, multi-event structure, perturbation-based hard cases, and validated 600-example coverage focused on when answers change.
